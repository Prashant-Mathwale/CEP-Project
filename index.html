In Case of an Accident

Immediate Steps

  1. Ensure Safety: Stop, turn on hazard lights, and move to a safe location.
  2. Check for Injuries: Assess everyone and call an ambulance immediately if needed.
  3. Call for Help: Contact the police (100/112) and highway helpline (1033).
  4. Document Scene: Take clear photos of vehicle damage, positions, and license plates.
  5. Exchange Information: Share and get names, contacts, and insurance details.
  6. File a Report: File an FIR and inform your insurance company promptly.

Emergency Numbers

  • 1033 (Highway)
  • 108 (Ambulance)
  • 100 (Police)
  • 112 (All-in-One)

Vehicle Insurance Explained

Types of Insurance Cover

Third-Party Liability

This is the minimum legal requirement. It covers injury, death, or property damage to a third party. It does not cover damage to your own vehicle.

Comprehensive Cover

Includes third-party liability plus "Own Damage" (OD) cover, which protects your vehicle against accidents, theft, and natural disasters. Highly recommended.

View More Details
Common Add-ons for Comprehensive Policies:
  • Zero Depreciation: Ensures you get the full claim amount for parts without deduction for depreciation.
  • Engine Protection: Covers damage to the engine, especially from water ingression during floods.
  • Roadside Assistance: Provides help for breakdowns, flat tyres, towing, etc.

Toll & Registration Essentials

Latest Mandates

FASTag Rules (Updated)

FASTag is mandatory for electronic toll collection. Recent rules emphasize:

  • One Vehicle, One FASTag: NHAI's initiative to discourage using a single FASTag for multiple vehicles or multiple FASTags for a single vehicle.
  • Complete Your KYC: To avoid deactivation, users must complete their 'Know Your Customer' (KYC) process by updating it with their bank.
  • Penalty for No FASTag: Entering a FASTag lane without a functional tag can result in paying double the toll amount in cash.
High-Security Registration Plate (HSRP)

Mandatory for all vehicles. These are tamper-proof aluminium plates that help prevent vehicle theft and misuse. They come with a color-coded sticker to identify the fuel type. Book them through authorized state portals.

Vehicle Modification Rules

Rule: As per CMVR 100, visibility for front/rear windshields must be at least 70%, and for side windows, at least 50%. The use of any kind of sun film is illegal.

Rule: Any aftermarket exhaust that increases noise beyond the stock level (typically 80-85 dB) is illegal. Pressure, multi-tone, or musical horns are strictly prohibited.

Tyres: Upsized tyres must not protrude beyond the wheel arches. Lights: Auxiliary lamps are allowed but must be covered when not in use. Flashing/colored lights are illegal. Color: Changing the vehicle's color requires RTO approval and an update on the RC.

EVs & Environment

Green Initiatives

Electric Vehicle (EV) Subsidies

The central FAME-II scheme and various state policies offer incentives for purchasing electric vehicles. Benefits change frequently, so always check official government portals for the latest details before making a purchase.

Pollution Under Control (PUC)

A PUC certificate is mandatory proof that your vehicle's emissions comply with environmental regulations. It is valid for 1 year for new vehicles, and must be renewed every 6 months for older vehicles.
